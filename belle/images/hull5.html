Code carved on wooden timber. The letter “T” indicates the timber is on the “tribord,” or starboard, side; the  number IIII denotes it as the fourth frame from the ship’s main mast. The letters and numbers carved on each timber indicated that La Belle had been built from a “ship kit”. Three hundred years later, these codes helped investigators take the ship apart, timber by timber, and reassemble it in correct order in the conservation laboratory.
