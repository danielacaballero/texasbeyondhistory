Photomicrographs of a pointed flake tool taken with binocular compound microscope.  In the top images, the haft element is shown; a), microplating wash with filled-in striations, typical of contact with wood; b) probable post-depositional trampling damage. The bottom set of images shows indications of rotary motion on the graver tip of the tool. This combined evidence indicates this tool was used as a hafted graver on soft to medium-soft material, such as deciduous wood. The microplating wash and striations in haft area suggest the handle was made of wood. Images by Marvin Kay.
