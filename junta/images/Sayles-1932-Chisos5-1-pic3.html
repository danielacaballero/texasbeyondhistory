“Collared” bedrock mortar photographed by Sayles at Chisos 5:1. The “collar” or relatively well-preserved perimeter around the mortar hole, noted by Sayles as “not eroded,” is thought by bedrock mortar experts today to reflect the absorption of organic plant residues (such as nut or seed fats). The residues are thought to make the rock less susceptible to erosion. This sort of pattern might be expected from an often-reused processing feature. TARL Archives.
