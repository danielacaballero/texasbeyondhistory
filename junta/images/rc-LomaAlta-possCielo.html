Circular structural outline that Kelley photographed (probably in 1939) and labeled as being “back from” the Loma Alta site. This resembles a Cielo complex house outline. He probably spotted this structure on the same mountain pediment upon which the Loma Alta pithouse village is perched on the far end overlooking the Rio Grande floodplain. In fact the site map shows an unexcavated isolated circular structure at the far northern part of the mapped site area. (See Loma Alta map.)

In his published dissertation, Kelley says that “facing the village ruin across the canyon” along the western edge of the “eastern wing of the mesa” were the “floors of circular lodges, surrounded by banked-up mesa gravels and fire-cracked stones.” One possible interpretation he mentions is that these could represent “the remains of a temporary encampment of some nomadic group”(Kelley 1986:81). The proximity of Cielo complex sites to La Junta villages suggests close relationships among hunter-gatherers and villagers. CBBS Archives. Sul Ross.
