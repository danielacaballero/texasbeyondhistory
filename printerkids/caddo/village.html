Explore a Caddo Village!

The year is 1200 A.D.—roughly 800 years ago! The area is the Pineywoods of what is now East Texas.

It's a sunny early autumn day—a perfect time to visit a Caddo village. Hundreds of people live here and work together, cooking, making tools and pottery, and preparing food to store for the cold winter months.

Explore the village by clicking with your "mouse." Look carefully and see what surprises you can find. Here are a few clues:

  • Can you see children playing with a deer? Is it really a deer? Click the picture and find out.
  • Several men are making stone tools, while another man nearby is making a very special object. Can you tell what it is?
    •
  • A Caddo leader is wearing a robe made of turkey feathers. The men with him have hardly any clothes on, but they are right in style. Check it out.
  • A group of hardworking women are busy preparing food. Did you know that the Caddo grew sunflowers so they could eat the seeds? Click on the scene to see what other kinds of food they grew.
  • Two women are pounding long sticks into a wooden container. What could they be doing? Click on them to get your answer.
    •
  • The woman carrying a pot of water on her head is wearing a very fine outfit. Check it out.

Pretend you are flying high above this village. Can you imagine how it looks from above? Click the picture for a bird's-eye view!
