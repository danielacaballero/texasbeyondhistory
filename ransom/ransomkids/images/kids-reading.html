Reading and Doing Lessons

In spite of all of the activity around him, John, just six years old, is concentrating on the book in his lap. Ransom Williams was no doubt proud of the fact that his children could all read and write even though he and Sarah could not. Prior to the Civil War, it was illegal to teach slaves to read and write in most Southern states.

The Williams children were most likely educated in the community schools, either in nearby Antioch or Manchaca. Education was valued highly by the African-American community who recognized that learning to read and write was an important part of being able to succeed economically and to participate fully in civic life. Although the county and state government were often unwilling to give much support to African-American schools, freedmen communities like Antioch found ways to build and fund educational opportunities for their children.
