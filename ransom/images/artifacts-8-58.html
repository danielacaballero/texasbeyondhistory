These artifacts are clothing accessories. Late nineteenth-century shirts did not have attached collars with buttons on them. The collar was a separate piece attached to a shirt using collar studs (a). Shirt cuffs often did not have buttons and were secured around the wrists using cuff links. A brass cuff link (b) with an ornate red glass jewel is an elaborate piece of men's ware that was probably worn on Sundays and special occasions. Several different buttonhooks (c) also were found at the farmstead, and these were used to assist with buttoning shoes, gloves, or other apparel.
