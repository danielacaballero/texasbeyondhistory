A nylon stocking is used to filter the clay. Clay is made up of tiny particles that, when suspended in water, can pass through fine mesh, leaving behind rocks and other impurities. Plains villagers may have used tightly woven baskets to strain clay. Once strained, the excess moisture is allowed to evaporate until the desired moisture level is reached. (This step is not essential, but improves the quality and predictability of the clay.)
