Special Objects, Special Times

The Caddi (Caddo leader) stands ready to preside over a special event in his village. He is holding a staff of polished stone. Look below to see some of the real staffs made by the Caddo..
This pendant is made of polished quartz crystal. The carved lines show a person with crossed arms. A cord once passed through the hole drilled where the mouth would be so it could be worn like a necklace.
Caddos smoked long-stemmed pipes filled with tobacco grown in their fields. This image shows the broken bowl and stem fragments found in an archeological site. Today we know that smoking is bad for your health, but it was an important ritual for the Caddos.
These polished stone staffs were carried by Caddo leaders as symbols of their rank and power. These two were found in the tombs of leaders at what is today Caddoan Mounds State Historic Site near Alto in east Texas.
A mysterious hand, cut from a thin sheet of native copper, was a symbol used hundreds of years ago in many related cultures in the southeastern United States.
We don't really know the meaning of quartz crystals used by the Caddo, but they are found in graves and were obviously important symbols.
Music was an important part of Caddo life. These flutes were made of hollow bird leg bones and were decorated with carved patterns, as shown in the enlarged view and drawing at top.
These carved and polished stones were used in a game called "chunkey," which was played on special occasions such as festivals..
