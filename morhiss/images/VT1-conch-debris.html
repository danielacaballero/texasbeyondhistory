Among the shell tools from Morhiss, a small assemblage of debitage attributed to the on-site manufacture of shell ornaments and tools was recovered. This figure shows two conch shell fragments, the interior columella with a portion of the inner whorl still attached and a fragment of the spire. Both are interior views. Note the straight edge on the lower left of each fragment. This edge is the result of using a combination of groove and snap to remove inner and exterior whorl segments for manufacture of shell pendants and one type of shell adze. The remaining broken edges reflect the use of direct percussion to break the shell, probably to gain access to suitable sections of whorl and columella for tool and ornament blanks.
