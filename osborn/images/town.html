Map of southern end of Bastrop circa 1920 showing Osborn farm and locations of structures. (1) Osborn tenant house (built ca. 1906; (2) George W. Jones house and log buildings; frame tenant house (4) barn and corral. Tracks for the Missouri, Kansas, and Texas Railway were laid through the Osborn property in the late 1800s. The farm encompassed 327 acres of fertile bottomland south of the town of Bastrop, just east of the juncture of Gills Branch Creek and the Colorado River.
