Neck-banded Utilitarian Jar
Frank Norris Farm (41RR2)
Post 1300 A.D

Height = 20 cm
Maximum Diameter = 16.5

This untyped utilitarian jar has light fingernail punctations running vertically around the narrow rim. These decorations would have been made while the clay was still malleable. In addition, the pot appears to be burnished, a type of polishing that takes place when the clay is leather hard.

This pot is from a place in Red River County called the Norris Farm that has been of interest to local collectors for many years. During the 1930's some of the site caved off into the Red River and burials were found and artifacts collected. A crew from the University of Texas, under the direction of B. B. Gardner did some test excavations there in summer of 1930. The context for this pot is unknown.
