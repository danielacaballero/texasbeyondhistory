Incised Square Bowl
A. J. Hatchel Site (41BW3)
ca. 1400-1600 A.D

Height = 5.9 cm
Maximum Body Diameter = 18.4 cm

The rim of this small bowl comes to peaks at each of the corners. It has a single incised line running horizontally under the rim. Two half-circular lines descend from single line on each side. There are three rows of punctations inside the half-circles. In addition, there are two rows of punctations in the center of the half-circles. The decorations is completed with fingernail punctuates running up each corner of the bowl. All the designs were made while the clay was still pliable. This bowl does not fit within any of the defined pottery types.

This piece was excavated from Burial Plot 1, Burial 14 at the Hatchel site in Bowie County, Texas on November 16, 1938. The site was excavated by a WPA crew under the direction of A. M. Woolsey of the University of Texas. This bowl was found on the north side of the burial, which was that of an adult.
