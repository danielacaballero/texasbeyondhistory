Fulton Aspect Duck Effigy Pot
Jasper Tucker or Mrs. Joe Watkins Farm (41AN44)
ca. 1400-1650 A.D

Height = 14.4 cm
Maximum Body Diameter = 18.7 cm

This is a simple coiled pot with a stylized duck head and tail applied to the rim of the pot while the clay is still malleable. There are two incised lines running horizontally under the rim of the pot. These line decorations were made when the pot was still in the green ware stage. White pigment (probably kaolin) was rubbed into the incised lines to highlight the design.

This pot was excavated from Burial AO-1 at the Tucker/Watkins Farm in Anderson County, Texas in November 1938. The site was worked by a crew from the University of Texas under the direction of A. M. Woolsey. The pot was found with the larger of two closely spaced burials that may be those of a mother and child. There were seven additional pottery vessels and five arrow points found in association with the larger grave. The small grave had two small ceramic pieces.
