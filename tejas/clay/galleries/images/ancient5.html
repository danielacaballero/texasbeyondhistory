Avery Engraved Compound Bowl
W. S. Russell Site (41TT7)
ca. 1430-1600 A.D

Height = 12.9 cm
Mouth Diameter = 15.5 cm

This compound bowl has a flaring rim and inverted shoulders over a carinated conical bowl. The entire vessel was covered with a thin red slip of clay through which the design was engraved to create a more dramatic effect. There are two engraved lines running horizontally around the rim. In addition, four sun symbols are engraved on the shoulder of the vessel with engraved panels acting as separators.

The Russell site in Titus County, Texas was excavated in August and September of 1930, by a crew from the University of Texas under the direction of B. B. Gardner. The site apparently had a sizable Late Caddo cemetery, but the skeletal materials were in such a poor state of preservation only three burials had human remains. Forty-two additional "caches" of ceramics and other artifacts were located, and assumed now to be burials lacking preserved skeletal remains. It is not known precisely where this bowl was found within the site.
