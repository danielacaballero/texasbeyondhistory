Fulton Aspect Rattle Bowl
Miss Mattie Gandy Farm Site (41FK4)
ca. 1430-1600 AD

Height = 4.5 cm
Mouth Diameter = 8 cm

This unusual small bowl was adorned while the paste was still wet. The decorations consist of four hollow nodes under the rim. In addition, there are two rows of appliquéd "buttons" of clay running in a band around the mouth and covering the nodes. The hollow nodes contain small hard objects, either clay pellets or pebbles, that make a rattling sound when shaken. Bowls of this type were likely used for special ceremonies.

The Gandy Farm site in Franklin County, Texas was excavated in June 1934 by a crew from the University of Texas under the direction of A. T. Jackson. The rattle pot was recovered from Burial R-1 and was accompanied by 11 additional pots, one celt and four arrow points. Most of the skeletal material was gone and there was no indication as to the sex or age of the deceased.
