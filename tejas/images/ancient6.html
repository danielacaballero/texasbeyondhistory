Engraved Bottle
Joe Justiss Site (41MX2)
ca. 1600-1680 AD

Height = 20.5 cm
Maximum Body Diameter = 10.4

This elaborately engraved bottle has an unusual form. The rim of the piece is slightly flared. There are four tube-like pieces that hold the two globular sections together. Scroll and spiral line work decorate the top section, while the four tubes and base section have varying engraved line work and cross-hatching. This vessel does not fall within any known ceramic types.

The Joe Justiss site is in Morris County, Texas, and was partially excavated from October 11 through October 16, 1930, by a crew from the University of Texas under the direction of A. T. Jackson. Eleven burials were uncovered. The engraved bottle was recovered from Burial 5 along with two other ceramic vessels and an arrow point. At the time of excavation, Mr. Jackson noted that the bottle was the only one of it's kind found in Texas although it was very similar to a few rare specimens found in Arkansas. No trace of human bone was found in this burial.
