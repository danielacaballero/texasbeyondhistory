Patton Engraved Bowl
Patton Site (41AN26)
ca. 1400-1650 AD

Height = 14cm
Mouth Diameter = 18 cm

The decorations on this large carinated bowl were completed after the vessel had been fired. This method of decoration is called engraving. The major design element consists of four curved concentric arms that interlock. The outside line of this element is ticked (it has tick marks) as are horizontal lines around the rim and the bottom of the carination. The bottom of the bowl is brushed.

This pot was found in Anderson County by landowner Richard Patton, who discovered the site due to erosion on his property. With the help of a neighbor, Mr. Patton soon dug up 12 burials and collected many ceramic pieces. Unfortunately, little information is known about the burials and their associations. The University of Texas purchased the collection of pots in 1933. A. T. Jackson later worked at the site in August of 1933 and re-excavated three of the burials to salvage some information.
