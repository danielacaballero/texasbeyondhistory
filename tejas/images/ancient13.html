Holly Fine Engraved Bottle
George C. Davis Site (41CE19)
ca. 900-1300 AD

Height 20.2 cm
Maximum Body Diameter 12.5

This beautifully executed bottle is decorated with typical motifs for this pottery type. There are 12 finely incised lines running horizontally around the rim, additional incised lines run around the top of the body and form a complex design made up of geometric elements. After the pot was fired, broad areas of the design were engraved to make the incised areas stand out. Red ochre rubbed into the lines and engraved areas greatly enhances the design. The pot was also polished.

This bottle was excavated in 1970 at the Davis site (also known as Caddoan Mounds State Historic Site) in Cherokee County, Texas by a crew from the University of Texas under the direction of Dr. Dee Ann Story. It was found … CHK ….variety of grave goods including ear spools, beads, a celt, arrow points and additional ceramics. Very little was left of the skeleton, although it is thought to be an adult.
