Holly Fine Engraved Bottle
Tom M. Cole Collection (41RR3)
ca. 900-1300 AD

Height = 19 cm
Maximum Body Diameter = 14.5 cm

This finely executed bottle is a good example of the engraving on pottery. Engraving is executed after the piece has been fired. The design is literally scratched out of the smooth surface of the vessel. In this bottle engraved lines run horizontally around the rim, as well as the top-most portion of the body. The principal design elements are a series of engraved spirals and concentric circles, the center portion of which has been excised. Red pigment has been rubbed into the engraved lines and creates a striking effect.

This bottle as well as several other pieces was purchased from Tom M. Cole in 1930. Mr. Cole took A. T. Jackson of the University of Texas out to view a mound in Red River County, Texas where he had dug a large pit. He discovered several skeletons in what appeared to be a multiple Early Caddo burial. No additional data was recorded.
