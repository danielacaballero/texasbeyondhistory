This detailed drawing is interpreted as “bringing boughs to the Sun Dance Lodge”. A mounted Indian man rides behind a group of people under umbrellas, who are pulling felled trees. The lodge is framed by upright poles or forked tree trunks and is adorned with feathers and bundles of goods (possible offerings).  Although identified in the German label as “Indian burial”, the structure and boughs are thought to identify it as a Sun Dance Lodge. Father Peter Powell identified the scene as Kiowa because Kiowa women, unlike Cheyenne, assisted in bringing boughs to the lodge. TARL Archives.
