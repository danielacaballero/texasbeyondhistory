TBH Lesson Plan

Lesson Title: Nacogdoches Timeline

Subject: Texas History

Grade levels: 4-7

Rationale: Students create a timeline to understand how long people have lived in the area near Nacogdoches, Texas.

Materials:

  • Student handout sheets, pens, pencils, and rulers

Lesson Duration: One 45 minute class period

Objectives:


Social Studies, 7th Grade

  • (2A) compare cultures of American Indians in Texas
  • (2B) identify important individuals, events, and issues related to European exploration and colonization of Texas
  • (21C) organize and interpret information from timelines
  • (21H) use appropriate mathematical skills to interpret social studies information
  • (22C) transfer information from one medium to another

Social Studies, 4th Grade

  • (1A) identify Native American groups in Texas
  • (1C) identify Spanish missions in Texas
  • (22C) organize information into timelines
  • (22F) use appropriate mathematical skills to interpret social studies information

Activity - Students work in pairs

Step 1: Put students into pairs.

Step 2: Students use handout to create timeline, either by hand or by using a free online timeline maker such as the one at www.readwritethink.org.

Step 3: When timelines are complete, students answer two synthesis questions, either in writing or orally.

Lesson Closure: Ask students to share their answers to the synthesis questions out loud to the whole class.

Modification: Use fewer items on the timeline.

Assessment: Completed timeline and appropriate answers to synthesis questions.
