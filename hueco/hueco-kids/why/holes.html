Why are there so many holes in the rocks?

It’s all about granite and gravity. The hills at Hueco Tanks are made of igneous rocks called poryhyritic syenite. This is a hard-to-pronounce name for volcanic rocks similar to granite. Over time, rainwater fell on the rocks and was carried downhill by gravity, carving out cracks, channels, and caves in the rock. Wind and blowing sand also has weathered out holes in the rocks.
