Map of Spanish presidio overlaid on magnetometer map of the site. Note how the space in the central part of the map correlates with the circular plaza area in the center of the presidio. Encircling the plaza area is a ring of structures that closely correlates with the ring of dipole anomalies in the magnetometer map. The magnetometer was sensing magnetic objects, such as nails, and other disturbances in the soil, in the trash piles outside the soldiers' huts. Archeologists determined that only one row of structures was actually built at the presidio, rather than the three rows shown in the Spanish map. Graphic courtesy of THC.
