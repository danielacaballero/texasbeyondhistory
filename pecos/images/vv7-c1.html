A burned rock midden being dug up by looters searching for artifacts. It is part of the Castle Canyon site (VV7) recorded by Graham and Davis in 1958). They focused on a rockshelter on the canyon wall above the midden that locals called "Painted Cave," a popular name for pictograph sites in many parts of the state. The photo from the ANRA-NPS archives dates to 1993 when the site(which lay above the maximum flood pool level of Amistad Reservoir) was revisited.
