Red ocher pebbles and pigment stainswere noted in or near several burials at Loma Sandia. Ground into a fine dust, the mineral pigment has been used throughout history for body ornamentation, wall painting, dyes for basketry, and other artistic/symbolic representations. Prehistoric peoples incorporated the bright red pigment into burial ceremonies, perhaps adorning the body before placement in the grave. The red stain shown at left is one of 17 documented at Loma Sandia; unlike this one, most stains were detected in direct contact with the human remains. Red ocher is an iron-rich mineral known as hematite. The pebbles at right are examples of the raw material. Left photo, UTSA-CAR archives; right photo, TARL archives.
