Clusters of offerings were placed in many of the graves at Loma Sandia. Usually tightly stacked together, these items—ranging from chipped-stone tools and projectile points to perforated shell pendants—may have been enclosed in leather pouches or fiber bags that deteriorated and disappeared over time. Most of these items are“ utilitarian” objects used in daily life, although many such objects were placed in graves while still in pristine shape, implying that they were “new” and unused. The cluster shown at top left included four Tortugas dart points, five adzes (distally beveled tools or gouges) probably used for woodworking, two chert cores, several flake tools, one hammerstone, a mano (handstone for grinding), and a piece of kaolin (pipestone). Black and white photos of some of these artifacts are shown: Tortugas dart points (top right and center row) and distally beveled tools (bottom row).  UTSA-CAR archives.
