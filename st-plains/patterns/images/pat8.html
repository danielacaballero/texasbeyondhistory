Body ornaments, such as shell pendants and necklaces, were found with burials and may have been worn by participants during ceremonies as well. Most were made from marine or freshwater mollusk shells, although hollow animal bones were also commonly used. Ornaments on the top row are of conch shell and are from the Olmos Dam cemetery site near San Antonio. The shells at top left have been perforated to create  cross-shaped designs. At right, conch shell columella have been made into pendants. At bottom left is a decorated conch shell pendant from Loma Sandia. At bottom right, a necklace or strand of bird bone beads from the Morhiss cemetery site in Victoria County. Image credits: top row, left, Thomas R. Hester; right, Curtis Dusek; bottom left, Cathy Dodt-Ellis; bottom right, TARL Archives.
