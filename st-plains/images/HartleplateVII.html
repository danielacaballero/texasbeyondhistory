Artifact plate from Hartle and Stephenson’s 1951 report on the Falcon Arroyo site. Of note are all the triangular dart points in the upper two photos and the range of variation; most of these would be classified as Tortugas dart points, but several have rounded bases and fit Abosolo points and several are more lanceolate, but do not appear to be Paleoindian styles. The two photos in the middle row include unifacial and bifacial adzes, known as Clear Fork tools (gouges or distally-beveled tools).
