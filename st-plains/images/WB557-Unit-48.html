Close-up view of a cluster of burned sandstone rocks at the Boiler site. Sandstone is a relatively porous sedimentary rock composed of compacted sand held together by silica. Sandstone rocks used in cooking absorb visually undetectable organic residues from both firewood and food materials. New analytic approaches are taking advantage of these preserved organic residues to identify diet and for radiocarbon dating. UTSA-CAR Archives.
