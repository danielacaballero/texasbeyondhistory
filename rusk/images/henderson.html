Hendrick and Ware Plantations Cultural Landscape

Henderson

Before and after the Civil War, farmers traveled to the Rusk County seat at Henderson to pay taxes and conduct real estate and commercial transactions. Approximately 20 miles southwest of the Hendrick and Ware plantations, the county seat boasted seven blacksmith shops, six wagon or carriage shops, three gristmills, two saddleries, two tin shops, two tailors, two printer/publishers, a jug factory, cabinet shop, brickyard, tannery, distillery, sawmill, and shoe shop. In 1860, the widow of Levi Hill Ware, Elizabeth Ware Flanagan, moved to Henderson with her second husband, James Winwright Flanagan. Several of the Hendrick's adult children lived and worked in Henderson as well.
