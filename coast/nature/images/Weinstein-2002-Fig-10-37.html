Bone tools from the Guadalupe Bay site. From top to bottom, a needle-point awl; a shaped and smoothed tool fragment made from a bison-sized long bone that may have been used as a pottery polishing tool; and a various perforated long bone and antler (lower right) of unknown function. Adapted from Weinstein 2002, Figures 10-36 and 10-37.
