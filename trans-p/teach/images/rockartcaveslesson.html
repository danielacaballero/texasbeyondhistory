TBH Lesson Plan

Lesson Title:
Rock Art Caves

Subjects: Social Studies, Art

Grade level: 4

Rationale: Students use the Internet to learn about rock art and the raw materials used to create them. Students work together in small groups to create caves for other students to explore in the classroom.

Materials:

  • Computers with Internet access.
  • Student Handout: Examples of rock art from Texas and the World pages 1-4; (pdf, 253k)
  • Brown paper, colored markers.

Lesson Duration: One class period

Texas Essential Knowledge and Skills (TEKS):7th Grade
Social Studies

  • 114.6 Social Studies (22A), differentiate between, locate, and use primary and secondary sources.
  • 114.6 Social Studies (22B), analyze information by drawing inferences.
  • 114.6 Social Studies (22C), organize and interpret information in visuals.

Art

  • 117.14 Art (2A), integrate ideas about self, life events, family and community in original artworks.
  • 117.14 Art (2B), design original artworks.
  • 117.14 Art (3B),compare and contrast selected artworks from a variety of cultural settings.

Activity

Step 1: Show students several examples of rock art: Rock Art of Texas and the World.

Step 2: Students explore the interactive: “Make Your Own Rock Art!” on Texas Beyond History to learn how prehistoric peoples created paint pigment and brushes using materials found in nature.

Step 3: Divide students into small groups and give each group several large sheets of brown paper and colored markers. The groups cover the “cave walls” with their own rock art designs.

Step 4: Tape the sheets of paper over desks or attach to the wall or bookcase to make a “cave” for each group. Rock art designs should be inside the cave.

Step 5: Groups explore the different caves and try to learn something of the culture portrayed by the rock art.

Student Product:Paper cave drawings

Extension Activity:

  • Have students explore the following rock art resources to learn more about types of art and the cultures that created them: “Artistic Expression” sections of the Trans-Pecos and Plateaus and Canyonlands exhibits on Texas Beyond History.
  • Kirkland, Forrest. (1967). The Rock Art of Texas Indians. Austin: University of Texas Press.
  • Shafer, Harry J. and Jim Zintgraff (1986). Ancient Texans: Rock Art and Life Ways Along the Lower Pecos. San Antonio: Texas Monthly Press.
  • Have students explore the interactive, “Mysteries of the Texas Desert: The People of Hueco Tanks” www.texasbeyondhistory.net/kids/hueco.html to learn more about the rock art of the Jornada-Mogollon culture.
