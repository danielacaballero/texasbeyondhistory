Historic photograph of a jacal and its occupants in the Redford area, ca. 1900-1910. Well into the 20th century, the people of La Junta (vicinity of present-day Presidio, Texas and Ojinaga, Mexico) used essentially the same materials and techniques to build their houses as the first La Junta villagers had, some 700 years earlier. CBBS Archives, Sul Ross.
