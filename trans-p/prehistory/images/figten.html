CBBS archaeologist examining the stratigraphic profile at an Early Archaic site. Two separate occupations were found here, one at about the level of the archaeologist’s head, the other towards his knees. These deeply buried occupations are 2000 years younger than the Paleoindian era. It is therefore easy to postulate that any Paleoindian locales would be buried even more deeply in desert basins such as this one. CBBS, Sul Ross.
