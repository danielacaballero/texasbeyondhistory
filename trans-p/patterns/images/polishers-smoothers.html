Tools of the potter. Clay vessels were formed, smoothed, and polished using simple tools: sherds from broken pots as well as small pebbles. Paint, made from pulverized minerals such as ocher and carbon, was mixed on a small “palette,” such as a sherd or flat rock. Shown at left are sherds used as smoothing tools. At right are two small pebbles used as smoothers and a palette bearing traces of pigment.
