Pictograph of a horned, plumed serpent in Jaguar Cave in Alamo Canyon. Both red and white paint is present, although the red is faded. This Jornada Mogollon motif, often likened to the Mesoamerican god, Quetzalcoatl, was executed in both pecked and painted designs in western Trans-Pecos sites. This image is a composite of "stitched" photos by Robert Mark, Rupestrian Cyberservices.
