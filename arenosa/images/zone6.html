Zone 6: Early Paleoindian Period

Zone 6 at Arenosa dates to the Early Paleoindian period, prior to 9400 B.C., which corresponds to Dibble's Bonfire subperiod. Zone 6 includes strata 39-42. There are no cultural remains or other definitive evidence of a human presence at Arenosa during this time, although one feature had tantalizing characteristic. Zone 6 was sampled only in a small area of the shelter floor in the deepest excavation during the final season.

The partial remains of a young adult male Bison antiquus (an extinct species of Late Pleistocene bison) were found in Stratum 40 and identified as Feature 18. The carcass exhibits an unusually high degree of disarticulation and is associated with two limestone cobbles that are likely too large to have been deposited at the site naturally. However, no definitive evidence of human involvement with the carcass, such as tools found in association with it, nor were cut marks found on the bones.

Two Bison antiquus long bone fragments were found amid a scatter of bone fragments from large ungulates in Stratum 42 and identified as Feature 19. As the case with Feature 18, there is no evidence of any kind of human modification of the bones. It is therefore unclear whether either of these features represent an early human presence at Arenosa.
Feature 18, the disarticulated partial remains of a young adult male Bison antiquus, was found in Stratum 40 and dates to sometime before 9000 B.C. It is unclear whether the bones represent an early human presence at Arenosa, but this is hinted at by the presence of two limestone cobbles that seem too large to have been transported to the site by nature.
