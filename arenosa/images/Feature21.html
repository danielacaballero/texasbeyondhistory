Feature 21 was a large pit near the sloping back of the shelter, which can be seen at the top of the photo. This pit contained 10 grinding slabs as well as ash, charcoal, stone, snail and mussel shell, and chipping debris from the manufacture of stone tools. It was dug from the surface of Stratum 9, through Stratum 10, into Stratum 11 and is a particularly clear example of how prehistoric peoples sometimes disturbed the natural stratigraphy. It is to be expected that not all such events left as tell-tale evidence as did Feature 21. ANRA-NPS Archives at TARL.
