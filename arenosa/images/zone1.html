Zone 1: Late Prehistoric and Historic Periods

Zone 1 at Arenosa dates to the Late Prehistoric and Historic periods, which lasted from A.D. 700 until the present. It encompasses the Flecha and Infierno subperiods defined by Dave Dibble. Zone 1 includes uppermost strata, of which only Stratum 2 contained cultural materials. Stratum 1 is a sterile flood deposit created by the 1954 flood that extends to the roof of the rockshelter.

Stratum 2 was a mixed deposit that included early to mid 20th-century historic materials, such as shell casings, bottle glass fragments, and assorted rusted metal scrap. It is no surprise that these materials became mixed into the sandy sediment containing the latest prehistoric evidence. Many critters, wild and domesticated alike, seek shelter in overhangs and dig holes, paw around and otherwise disturb sediment. Still, the bulk of the artifacts and materials from Stratum 2 clearly date to the prehistoric era.

Bone tools and beads were not very common in Stratum 2, but most of the beads that were recovered are incised with patterns. Neither groundstone nor painted pebbles are very common, either. All of the arrow points from Arenosa come from Stratum 2 and consist of Perdiz, Toyah, and Harrell arrow points, among others. Ensor dart points were found intermingled with the arrow points, suggesting some admixture of Terminal Archaic materials.

Stratum 2 contained only one feature. Feature 8 was a pit that contained several fragments of limestone, charcoal, snail shell, and a large grinding slab, used to such a degree on both faces that a hole had been worn completely through the slab.
