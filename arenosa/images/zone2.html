Zone 2: Terminal Late Archaic Period

Zone 2 at Arenosa dates to the Terminal Late Archaic period, which lasted from 400 B.C. until A.D. 700. It encompasses the Flanders and Blue Hills subperiods defined by Dave Dibble. Zone 2 includes strata 3-9, of which strata 3-5, 7, and 9 contained cultural materials.

Zone 2 contained by far the largest number and variety of artifacts at Arenosa. Many of them come from a living surface identified in Stratum 9. Zone 2 also yielded the highest number of artifacts made of antler and bone, including some bone tools and beads that have been incised with patterns. The vast majority of the site’s painted pebbles and much of its groundstone also come from Zone 2, as well as many of its dart points. Shumla points are prevalent in Stratum 9, but Frio, Ensor, and Marcos points dominate the rest of the strata. Other artifacts of note include a quartzite bead and a fragment of an incised pipe.

Zone 2 also contained an impressive number of features. Features 1 and 9 are large limestone spalls with mortar holes worn into their upper surfaces. Features 2, 4, and 7 are hearths that consist of concentrations of charcoal surrounded by burned fragments of limestone. Feature 6 is a large deposit of ash. Features 20 and 21 are pits that contain grinding slabs as well as ash, charcoal, stone, snail and mussel shell, and chipping debris from the manufacture of stone tools. Feature 12 is a burial pit that contains the remains of a single individual accompanied by several grinding slabs and a mano (handstone), and topped with red ochre.
