Feature 18 consists of the disturbed, partial remains of a young adult male Bison antiquus (an extinct species of late Pleistocene bison). They were found in stratum 40 and date to sometime before 9000 B.C. The carcass exhibits an unusually high degree of dismemberment and is associated with two limestone cobbles that are likely too large to have been deposited at the site naturally. However, there is no direct evidence of human involvement with the carcass, such as tools found in association with it or cut marks on the bones. It is therefore unclear whether the remains represent an early human presence at Arenosa. ANRA-NPS Archives at TARL.
