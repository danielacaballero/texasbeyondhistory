Arenosa's deposits are 48 feet thick and contain 49 identifiable strata (layers). The light-colored strata are sterile deposits created when the Pecos and Rio Grande rivers flooded the site. The dark-colored strata are cultural deposits containing charcoal, burned rock, mussel shell, animal bone, broken tools, and other cultural materials left behind by the people who camped at the site in between floods. ANRA-NPS Archives at TARL.
