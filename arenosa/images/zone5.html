Zone 5: Early Archaic and Late Paleoindian Periods

Zone 5 at Arenosa dates to the Early Archaic and Late Paleoindian periods, which lasted from 9400 until 4300 B.C. It encompasses the Oriente and Veijo subperiods defined by Dave Dibble. Zone 5 includes strata 32-38, of which strata 38, 36, 34, and 32 contained cultural materials.

The strata of Zone 5 were repeatedly flooded by the Pecos and Rio Grande rivers, so no perishable materials were preserved in them. Artifacts that were found include chipped stone tools, groundstone, burned rock, and snail and mussel shell. Bone was poorly represented and very poorly preserved. The earliest direct evidence of human occupation of Arenosa consists of 4 unifaces, several flakes, charcoal, and snail and mussel shell recovered from Stratum 38. Radiocarbon assays taken from the charcoal date this occupation to 8990 B.C. (calibrated mid-point).

Dart points were found in strata 36, 34, and 32 and consisted mainly of Baker, Bandy, Pandale, and a type referred to by Dibble as “Early Barbed” points. Stratum 34 contained a concentration of burned rock and Stratum 32 contained 28 pieces of groundstone—more than any other single stratum at Arenosa.
