Feature 7 consisted of a concentration of burned rocks that was described as representing the remnants of a hearth that had been lined by thin slabs on one side (to the right in the photo) perhaps representing a windbreak to protect the fire. Judging from the photo, this field interpretation seems unlikely. The mass of rocks looks more like a rock-filled pit. The thin slabs (only one of which is visible in the photo and it juts toward the photographer and is somewhat out of focus) seem more likely to be lining a separate, earth-filled pit. This illustrates the need for a thorough reappraisal of the site and the challenge of trying to make sense of complex stratigraphy and incomplete records. ANRA-NPS Archives at TARL.
