Feature 9 was a large limestone stream-rounded rock with three mortar holes worn into its upper surface. The mortars would have been used with pestles (likely pointed river cobbles) to grind things like seeds. The feature sat on a living surface identified at the top of Stratum 9, which dates to about 280 B.C. It is surrounded by a charcoal concentration, snail and mussel shell, and bone (including fish vertebrae). ANRA-NPS Archives at TARL.
