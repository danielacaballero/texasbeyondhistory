The leaves of plants such as sotol were an excellent source for the strong fiber used in ways by early peoples. The leaves were dried and woven into matting, sandals, and baskets, or were split and pulled into thin threads to make twined cord. The fiber cording, was used to fashion netting, rope, and many other necessary items.
Cords made from twisted plant fibers were used for many different needs. This specimen was found in 41VV191. From the ANRA-NPS collections at TARL.
