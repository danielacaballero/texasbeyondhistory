Flat sections of mussel shell were cut, shaped, and drilled with a hole to create these objects. Likely they were strung on cords and worn as pendants. They were found at Kincaid Shelter in Uvalde County. TARL collections.
These beads were made from hollow bone that was deeply grooved with a sharp tool and then snapped into sections. The ends of the beads were then smoothed by abrasion. From the ANRA-NPS collections at TARL.

Prehistoric peoples fashioned ornaments and jewelry from a number of natural items as shown in these specimens. Rather than mere adornments, there is little doubt that some “ornaments” had social and symbolic meanings. For instance, large pendants of exotic materials may have symbolized authority and been worn only by certain members of society, perhaps male leaders.
This unusual bracelet was made from beads of grooved and snapped bone, mussel shell, and small seeds, as well as unidentified plant material woven into the mix. The ornaments are still strung on the original fiber cording. It was found in Conejo Shelter (41VV162) in Val Verde. From the ANRA-NPS collections at TARL.
