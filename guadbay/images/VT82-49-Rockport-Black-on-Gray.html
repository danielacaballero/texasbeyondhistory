This partially reconstructed deep, globular bowl with a constricted neck from the Linn Lake area presents analysts with a typological conundrum. It has rather sloppily applied asphaltum paint, thus could be classified as Rockport Black-on-gray, yet it also has elements of Rockport Incised including two incised lines and a notched or “nicked” rim. Avocational archeologist Bill Birmingham collected the sherds over a 27-year period as he monitored an eroding site and was able to piece together most of the vessel. On display at the Museum of the Coastal Bend, photo by Steve Black.
